Suctor

The core component of a suction machine is the suction pump. It generates a vacuum that creates suction to remove fluids. These pumps can be either electrically powered or manually operated. Suctioned fluids are collected in a container attached to the suction machine. This container can be disposable or reusable, depending on the model. Many suction machines have filters to prevent fluids from entering the pump and contaminating it. These filters need regular maintenance or replacement.

Trenchers

These are the most common type of trencher and are suitable for digging trenches in relatively soft to moderately hard soil. They have a large wheel with a digging chain or teeth that rotates, cutting into the ground as it moves forward. Chain trenchers are designed for cutting trenches using a chain with digging teeth. They can be used in a variety of soil conditions, including rocky terrain. Chain trenchers are often used for digging deeper trenches.

Dragline

Draglines are used in large-scale civil engineering projects, such as the construction of reservoirs, canals, and major infrastructure projects. They can be employed to remove contaminated soil or materials during environmental cleanup efforts. In some cases, draglines are adapted for use in marine environments, such as for dredging rivers, lakes, or ports. Draglines can vary significantly in size, with some of the largest models weighing thousands of tons and having a bucket capacity measured in cubic meters.

Crawlers

Crawler machines are characterized by the use of continuous tracks, which are a series of interconnected metal or rubber belts that wrap around a set of wheels or sprockets. These tracks distribute the machine's weight over a larger surface area, reducing ground pressure and providing better stability and traction, especially in challenging terrain. They can be employed to remove contaminated soil or materials during environmental cleanup efforts.

Backhow loaders

The rear portion of the backhoe loader is essentially a tractor, with wheels or tires for mobility. The tractor unit provides power to both the loader and the excavator components. On the front of the machine, there's a loader bucket, similar to what you'd find on a front-end loader. This bucket is used for tasks like scooping and lifting materials, such as soil, gravel, or construction debris.

Buldozers

The most distinctive feature of a bulldozer is its large, wide blade at the front. This blade can vary in size and design but is typically heavy and angled to push material, such as soil, rocks, or debris. Bulldozers move on continuous tracks (crawler tracks) instead of wheels. These tracks provide excellent traction and distribute the machine's weight over a larger surface area, which helps prevent sinking into soft or uneven terrain.
